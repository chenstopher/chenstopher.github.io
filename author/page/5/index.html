Netflix Price Increase

12 July 2011

Netflix announced that they're gonna get rid of the DVD + Streaming plan. Instead, it's going to be two separate plans for DVD and Streaming. Plus if you want both DVDs and Streaming, it's going to end up costing about \$5 more than it used to.

This sucks.

I have the 2 DVD out + streaming plan. My monthly fee is gonna jump from \$15 to \$20. And what do I get for that extra 5 dollars? Nothing (yet).

I understand why they need to increase the price though. Netflix's content licensing fees are going to increase to almost \$2 billion from the \$180 million they were paying in 2010.

Hopefully, stuff like crappy movie selection, super low volumes, no bonus features, super small HD library,  crappy audio+video and all the problems with Instant Streaming will be fixed with the extra 5 bucks I'll be paying each month.

Left, right, or over?

19 April 2011

There is game I like to play when walking to and from places. It doesn’t have a name… or a point, but it’s something to do while I walk.

The Game

Pick out an object a few feet in front of your path. It can be a stick, puddle, or crack. Whatever. Just pick one out.

Then guess whether you will step on it with your left foot or your right foot.

If you guess correctly, you win and you get to say “FUCK YEAH” to yourself, in your head.

If you guess incorrectly, you lose and you only get to say “FUCK”.

And that’s it.

Observations

Here are two related observations I’ve made while playing this game:

  1. You will come up with a guess you are pretty sure of within a second. It will probably be correct.
  2. If it takes more than a second or you can’t make up your mind, you will step over the object.

There’s probably some scientific term for that second phenomenon but I don’t feel like researching it.

So that’s it. This is the type of stuff I come up with when I’m bored.

Cost per class

14 March 2011

I was wondering how much it would cost if I missed a day of class.

If you don't count the fees, housing, and meal plan:
costPerClass = \$2,485 tuition / (5 courses * 15 weeks * 2 classes per week per course)
costPerClass == \$16.57

If you do count the fees, housing, and meal plan:
costPerClass = \$8,250 tuition / (5 courses * 15 weeks * 2 classes per week per course)
costPerClass == \$55

Hmm, it was actually less than I thought it would be.
